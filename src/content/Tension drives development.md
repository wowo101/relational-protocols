[[Norms are constructed through committed practice|Commitment-structures inherently generate tensions]] between their explicit content and their actual effects. A consent protocol promises equivalence but in practice some voices carry more weight. A resource-sharing protocol promises solidarity but in practice some contributions go unrecognised. These tensions are not design flaws. They are where the architecture does its work — *if* they are held rather than suppressed or resolved too quickly.

## Feedback, tension, conflict

The architecture currently names two domains for processing what arises in collective life: [[Feedback and learning|feedback and learning]] (surfacing information) and [[Conflict engagement|conflict engagement]] (processing rupture). Between them sits the most generative space:

**Feedback** surfaces information — what's happening, what patterns are emerging, what the network is experiencing. This is descriptive: the network learning to see itself.

**Tension** arises when that information reveals a pull in two directions. Something a protocol promises and something it produces are in conflict. Two goods are in competition. An asymmetry is becoming visible but hasn't yet produced specific harm. Tension is not yet conflict — no specific incident, no named parties — but it is more than information. It has directionality and energy.

**Conflict** emerges when a tension involves specific harm, specific parties, or has been suppressed long enough to rupture relationship. The [[Conflict-as-information|conflict engagement protocols]] treat this as information and energy, feeding it back into the network's structural learning.

The escalation from feedback through tension to conflict is not inevitable. Tension that is held — named, explored, used to [[Diagnosis and construction inform protocol evolution|generate new understanding]] — drives protocol evolution without becoming conflict. Tension that is suppressed or ignored accumulates until it ruptures. The orientation layer's primary function is to create the conditions under which tensions can be held: surfaced without being instrumentalised, explored without being forced to premature resolution.

## Tensions within and between protocols

Tensions arise not only within protocols but between them. [[Design for asymmetry]] pulls against [[Start with simple rules]]. [[Make voice cheap]] pulls against [[Design for scale]]. [[Facilitation before formalisation]] pulls against the urgency of having something to adopt now. These inter-principle tensions are not bugs in the design — they are the architecture's immune system. A framework without internal tensions has stopped thinking.

The orientation layer is what prevents these tensions from collapsing into either pole. It doesn't resolve the tension between asymmetry and simplicity; it creates the conditions under which a group can ask, in this specific situation, which pull matters more — and revisit that judgement when circumstances change.

## Holding without extracting

The non-extractive framing matters. You don't *harness* tensions — you *hold* them. The orientation layer doesn't extract insight from discomfort; it creates conditions where what is uncomfortable can speak without being instrumentalised. The practice layer's somatic work is precisely the capacity to remain in contact with tension without rushing to resolve it. This is what distinguishes the architecture from optimisation frameworks that treat every tension as a problem to be solved — and it is why the [[Every protocol has three inseparable layers|three layers must be inseparable]]. Without practice, you lack the embodied capacity to stay with tension. Without orientation, you lack the reflective space to let it speak. Without interface, the tension has nowhere determinate to land.
