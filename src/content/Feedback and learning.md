The feedback and learning domain governs how the network learns from its own experience. It includes protocols for:

**Story harvesting** — how experience is shared and sense-made across the network.

**Structural review** — how operational learning feeds into governance evolution.

**Retrospectives** — how groups reflect on their own health. Periodically, any group that has adopted governance protocols reviews not just its outputs but its process: Are the agreements still fit for purpose, or have conditions changed? Have new tensions surfaced that require new agreements? Every agreement carries a review date; at review, the group asks whether the agreement still serves its driver, whether the driver itself has changed, and whether the compass layer is actually being practised. Where the full [[Collective decision-making]] cluster is adopted, this becomes a [[Circles are the basic unit of shared governance|circle]] retrospective — the scheduled moment when the circle turns its attention from *what we are doing* to *what our doing is doing to us*. See also [[Naming experiences is how protocols learn]].

Each protocol requires ritual and compass layers attending to: whether feedback practices are extractive, whose knowledge counts, and whether the network is learning or merely reporting. This domain is sketched at the principles level; its specific [[Every protocol has three inseparable layers|three-layer]] protocols remain to be developed.
