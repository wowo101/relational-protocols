A protocol that only specifies rules will produce bureaucracy; one that only cultivates practices will produce good intentions without accountability. Requiring all three layers – [[Every protocol has three layers|interface, practice, and orientation]] – in every protocol is what makes the interruption of separation structural rather than aspirational. A protocol missing any layer cannot be adopted.
