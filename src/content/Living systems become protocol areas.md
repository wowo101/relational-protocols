The original seven living systems – resource distribution, decision-making, feedback and learning, conflict engagement, information flow, care and support, connection and community – do not disappear in a protocol-based architecture. They become *areas* within which clusters of related protocols operate.

A protocol area is a thematic area of relational life – the space within which a cluster of related protocols operates. When agents adopt the [[Collective decision-making]] cluster around a shared domain, the governance structure that emerges is a [[Circles are a basic unit of shared governance|circle]]. But the protocol area concept is prior to the circle concept: areas exist wherever protocols cluster, whether or not the agents involved have adopted circle-based governance.

Two areas have been developed as full worked examples with specific [[Protocols|protocols]]:

- [[Collective decision-making]]
- [[Conflict engagement]]

Five areas have been sketched at the principles level:

- [[Resource distribution]]
- [[Feedback and learning]]
- [[Information flow]]
- [[Care and support]]
- [[Connection and community]]

The specific protocols in these areas remain to be developed through the process described in [[Protocol stewardship is distributed]].
