Part of the [[Collective decision-making]] domain.

**Interface.** Key roles rotate on defined terms with overlap periods where the outgoing and incoming holders work together. This governs the relationship between role-holders and those they serve, preventing the accumulation of informal authority in individuals.

**Practice.** During handover, the outgoing holder shares not only tasks and information but their felt experience of the role – what it cost, what it opened, what they would do differently. This creates a culture of honest reflection about power and burden.

**Orientation.** At each rotation, the group asks: Has this role become a "gravity well" that attracts more authority than intended? What does the pattern of who holds this role reveal about our assumptions?
