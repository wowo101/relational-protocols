We are embedded in systems of power that distribute resources and opportunities very unequally. That's what all of our designs need to respond to – and interrupt.

Aspiration doesn't produce equity; [[Interrupt separation at the level of structure|structure]] does. This means building explicit structural mechanisms into the [[Every protocol has three inseparable layers|accord layer]] of every protocol: inverse weighting (smaller organisations' voices count more, not less), caps on accumulation (no agent holds mandates across too many domains), mandatory "who is not in the room" checks before network-wide decisions, collective pre-emptive boundary-setting, and automatic redistribution triggers when accumulation exceeds consent-defined thresholds.

In addition, the [[Every protocol has three inseparable layers|compass layer]] of our protocols needs to make sure we regularly interrogate these structures: How do they actually distribute access, power, opportunity, labour? Where do we need to change them in order to interrupt asymmetry?
