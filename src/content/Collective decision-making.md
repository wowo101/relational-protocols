The collective decision-making domain governs how groups within Fabric make decisions on behalf of or affecting others. It is one of the most developed [[Living systems become protocol domains|protocol domains]], with five specific protocols each carrying all [[Every protocol has three inseparable layers|three layers]].

When a group of agents adopts these protocols together around a shared domain, the governance structure that emerges is what we recognise as a [[Circles are a basic unit of shared governance|circle]] – a self-governing group with a defined domain. Circles are not a precondition for these protocols; they are a consequence of enacting them together.

- [[Consent-as-default]].
- [[Mandate clarity]].
- [[Open decision records]].
- [[Tending shared roles]].
- [[Weaving between circles]].

Together these protocols address the core tensions in distributed governance: ensuring that no single voice is overridden while no single preference can block progress, that authority is always visible and bounded, and that the informal accumulation of power is surfaced and interrupted.
