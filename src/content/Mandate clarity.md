Part of the [[Collective decision-making]] domain.

Adoption of this protocol means accepting Fabric-specific responsibilities. In other words, adopters of this protocol become part of the network area that corresponds to the idea of a "core network" (independent organisations) and Fabric as an organisation (roles, circles, working groups).

**Interface.** The adopting node articulates what it is responsible for and empowered to decide (its domain) – and makes this legible to the rest of the network. When one node's decision affects another's domain, the affected node is consulted. Mandates are set by consent within the broader network, and changing a mandate requires re-engaging those affected.

**Practice.** Image-based strategy sensing, adapted from the ZRM's needs phase: at the start of a mandate process, participants select images based on somatic response rather than analytical judgment, then explore the collective pattern (Storch & Krause 2017). This bypasses the analytical filtering that tends to reproduce existing narratives and accesses pre-verbal, body-based knowing about what a group's mandate actually *is* – not just what it says on paper.

**Orientation.** Periodically, each node adopting this protocol examines its mandate in relation to power: Has its domain become a site of accumulation? Who benefits from the current boundaries? Whose work falls through the gaps between mandates?
